Chemical Engineers in Uranium Mining

Chemical Engineering in the Uranium Mining Industry

How Uranium is Mined

  To mine uranium, a method known as In Situ Leaching (ISL) is used. This leaves the rock in the ground, and obtains the minerals by dissolving them in a solvent. This method has been proven to be better for the environment, because it disturbs less ground. Uranium ISL dissolves the uranium in native groundwater mixed with an oxidant. Then, it is sent to a mill to be turned from a liquid into ore.

 The mill dissolves the uranium in acid, then applies multiple chemical processes to purify the uranium. Next, the uranium gets turned into uranium trioxide, pictured as the jar labeled "UO3" on the right. The powder then gets converted into uranium dioxide, compressed into pellets, and baked. Lastly, zirconium fuel tubes are attached and capped to the pellets, which makes it a uranium fuel rod.

Roles for Chemical Engineers

Wellfield Engineer

Responsibility

 Wellfield engineers are responsible for planning wellfield projects to maintain wellfields and dig new ones. Injection wells are holes dug to the ore that allow liquid to be injected, so it can dissolve the uranium. Wellfield engineers must find the optimal way to perform these tasks, considering monetary cost, time, and environmental concerns.

Employment Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering or Chemical Engineering
  • 4+ Years of Schooling
  • 5+ Years of Experience

Salary

$137,000 per year

Process Engineer

Responsibility

 The job of process engineers is to plan how to implement the theory of how to turn uranium ore into metal, then implement it. After designing the process, they obtain the appropriate machines, design quality assurance testing, and validate the process' output. They are also responsible for improving the chemical processes and products, and increasing the plant’s reliability and efficiency.

Employment Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Metallurgical Engineering or Chemical Engineering
  • 4+ Years of Schooling

Salary

$101,000 per year

Possible Employers

Cameco

 Cameco is one of the world's largest uranium fuel suppliers. They produce more than 30 million pounds of uranium concentrates annually, and have millions of pounds more of mineral reserves. They are also one of Canada's largest employers of Indigenous people, and are primarily based in Saskatchewan.

Denison Mines

 Denison Mines is a uranium exploration and development company focused in Northern Saskatchewan. It was founded by Stephen B. Roman, and has since diversified into coal, potash, and more.
